Green Energy Saving Tips

Shutting off a standing pilot light in seasons that you are not using your fireplace can save you upwards of $13.00 per month.
As some of the by-products from the chemical reaction of (the combustion of natural gas) are calcium, phosphate, and sodium, when the
fireplace is on, these byproducts have less chance to settle on the interior of the fireplace and exit the firebox with the rest of the 
flue gases.

When the only thing burning in the firebox of a direct-vent firebox is the pilot light, the pilot light many times does not have 
enough heat to push the calcium, phosphate, and sodium out of the firebox, up the chimney and into the atmosphere, so it settles on the interior 
of the firebox, logs walls and glass.

Cleaning your glass once a year should prevent it from staining. Always use a recommended gas glass cleaner. The cost is between $10 & $20 
and will last years. The replacement cost of stained glass will be minimum $300, plus labour, gaskets, etc..
